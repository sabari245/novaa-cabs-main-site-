How to maintain your rental car for optimal performance

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details
Written by admin
January 25, 2026 8 min read

Renting a car gives you the freedom to explore, but proper maintenance is key to ensuring a smooth and safe journey. Whether you're on a weekend getaway or an extended road trip, these essential tips will help you keep your rental car in optimal condition throughout your rental period.

Fuel & Fluid Checks

Fluid Checks & Maintenance

Check the engine oil level at least once during your rental period, especially for longer trips:

  • Park on level ground and wait for the engine to cool
  • Locate the dipstick, pull it out, and wipe it clean
  • Reinsert fully, then pull out to check the level
  • Add oil if needed (consult the owner's manual for type)

Monitor Coolant & Washer Fluid

Keep an eye on these essential fluids for optimal performance:

  • Check coolant levels when the engine is cool
  • Top up windshield washer fluid regularly
  • Look for any visible leaks under the vehicle
  • Report any significant fluid loss to RentQ immediately

Tire Maintenance

Tire Pressure Monitoring

Proper tire pressure is crucial for safety and fuel efficiency:

  • Check pressure when tires are cold for accurate reading
  • Use the recommended PSI found on the driver's door jamb
  • Inspect for uneven wear or damage
  • Don't forget to check the spare tire

Tread Depth Inspection

Ensure your tires have adequate tread for safe driving:

  • Use the penny test - insert upside down in tread
  • If you can see all of Lincoln's head, tread is too low
  • Look for signs of uneven wear or damage
  • Report any tire issues immediately

Cleaning & Interior Care

Regular Cleaning Practices

Keep your rental car clean for a more enjoyable journey:

  • Remove trash daily to prevent odors
  • Use floor mats to protect carpeting
  • Clean spills immediately to prevent stains
  • Avoid smoking in the vehicle

Important Cleaning Note

While basic cleaning is encouraged, avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that could damage the vehicle's interior. For major spills or stains, contact RentQ support for guidance.

Basic Mechanical Checks

Battery & Electrical Systems

Ensure your rental starts reliably every time:

  • Listen for slow cranking when starting
  • Check that all lights are functioning properly
  • Test windshield wipers and washers
  • Report any electrical issues immediately

Air Filter Maintenance

For extended rentals, check the air filter condition:

  • Locate the air filter housing under the hood
  • Inspect for excessive dirt or debris
  • Only attempt cleaning if you're comfortable
  • Contact RentQ for replacement if needed
